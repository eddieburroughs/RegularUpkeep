# 1. Homeowner Guide: How to Capture the Issue

Good photos help providers understand your problem, give accurate quotes, and complete work faster. Poor photos lead to misquotes, wasted trips, and disputes.

## The 4-Photo Rule

For every issue, capture these four shots:

| Shot | Purpose | Distance |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Context Shot** | Shows where the issue is located in the room/area | 6-10 feet back |
| **Medium Shot** | Shows the affected area clearly | 3-4 feet |
| **Close-Up** | Shows the specific problem in detail | 1-2 feet |
| **Reference Shot** | Shows model/serial numbers, measurements, or labels | As close as readable |

## Lighting Guidelines

### Do's
- Use natural daylight when possible (open blinds/curtains)
- Turn on all room lights
- Use your phone's flashlight for dark spaces (cabinets, attics, crawlspaces)
- Position yourself so light shines ON the problem, not behind it

### Don'ts
- Don't shoot directly into windows or lights (creates silhouettes)
- Don't use flash on reflective surfaces (mirrors, chrome, water)
- Don't take photos in complete darkness without a light source

### Lighting Quick Fixes

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Too dark | Add a lamp or flashlight nearby |
| Too bright/washed out | Turn off overhead light, use side lighting |
| Glare on surface | Change your angle, turn off flash |
| Shadows hiding detail | Add light from opposite direction |

## Distance & Angles

### For Wall/Ceiling Issues
- Stand perpendicular to the surface
- Include at least 2 feet of surrounding area for context
- For cracks: shoot along the crack AND perpendicular to it

### For Floor Issues
- Shoot from standing height looking down (context)
- Kneel for medium shot
- Get low/close for detail shots

### For Appliances & Equipment
- Full front view (context)
- Problem area (medium)
- Model/serial label (close-up)
- Control panel or error codes if applicable

### For Outdoor Issues
- Wide shot showing property context
- Medium shot of affected area
- Close-up of damage/problem
- Include reference objects for scale (ruler, coin, hand)

## Including Measurements

When to include measurements:
- Holes, cracks, or damage (show length/width)
- Spaces where something will be installed
- Areas with water damage (show extent)
- Any issue where size affects pricing

### How to Show Scale

| Method | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| Tape measure in frame | Cracks, holes, dimensions |
| Ruler next to damage | Small defects, gaps |
| Credit card for scale | Small damage, insect size |
| Hand in frame | Quick reference |
| Standard object | Door, window, furniture nearby |

## Model & Serial Number Tags

**Always photograph these for:**
- HVAC systems (furnace, AC, heat pump)
- Water heaters
- Appliances needing repair
- Garage door openers
- Pool/spa equipment

**Where to find them:**
| Equipment | Label Location |
|-----------|----------------|
| Furnace | Inside access panel, front or side |
| AC unit | Side panel, near electrical connection |
| Water heater | Front label or side panel |
| Refrigerator | Inside door jamb or back panel |
| Washer/Dryer | Inside door or back panel |
| Dishwasher | Inside door edge or side panel |

**Photo tips for labels:**
- Fill the frame with the label
- Ensure all text is readable (not blurry)
- If label is damaged, photograph what's visible
- Note: We can often look up parts from model numbers

## Safety Warnings

### STOP and Call 911 First If You See:

| Hazard | Signs | Do NOT Photograph |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| **Gas Leak** | Rotten egg smell, hissing sound | Leave immediately, call from outside |
| **Electrical Fire** | Smoke, burning smell, sparks | Evacuate, don't touch anything |
| **Structural Collapse** | Sagging ceiling, cracking walls | Leave the area |
| **Flooding with Power** | Standing water near outlets | Do not enter water |

### Photograph Safely:

| Situation | Safety Precaution |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Electrical issues | Do NOT touch; photograph from distance |
| Water leaks | Turn off water main first if active |
| Roof/heights | Do NOT climb; photograph from ground |
| Mold | Do not disturb; photograph as-is |
| Pest infestation | Do not handle; photograph from distance |

### When Photographing Hazards:
1. Stay at safe distance
2. Do not touch or move anything
3. Note the hazard in your description
4. Mark request as "Urgent" if active danger

## Video Guidelines

### When Video is Better Than Photos:
- Intermittent issues (noise that comes and goes)
- Water leaks (shows flow rate)
- HVAC issues (shows airflow, sounds)
- Mechanical failures (shows movement/lack thereof)
- Electrical issues (shows flickering, sparking)

### Video Requirements:
- **Duration:** 10-30 seconds
- **Orientation:** Horizontal (landscape) preferred
- **Stability:** Hold phone steady, use both hands
- **Audio:** Include sound if relevant (describe while filming)
- **Lighting:** Same rules as photos

### What to Show in Video:
1. Start wide (context)
2. Slowly zoom/move to the problem
3. Hold on problem area for 5+ seconds
4. Narrate: "This is [location]. You can see [problem]. It started [when]."

# 2. Provider/Handyman Documentation Standards

Proper documentation protects you in disputes, proves work quality, and builds your professional reputation.

## Required Photo Sets

### BEFORE Photos (Minimum 2)

**Purpose:** Document initial condition before any work begins

| Shot | What to Include | Why |
|------|-----------------|-----|
| Context | Full area showing location in room/property | Proves you were at correct location |
| Problem | Clear view of the issue to be addressed | Matches customer's complaint |

**Must Be Visible:**
- Date/time (embedded in metadata - automatic)
- The problem as described by customer
- Surrounding area condition
- Any pre-existing damage (document this!)

### DURING Photos (Minimum 1)

**Purpose:** Show work in progress and methodology

| Shot | What to Include | Why |
|------|-----------------|-----|
| Work in Progress | Active repair/installation | Proves work was actually performed |
| Discoveries | Anything found during work | Justifies change orders |

**Must Be Visible:**
- Materials being used
- Work methodology
- Any surprises/discoveries
- Access areas opened

### AFTER Photos (Minimum 2)

**Purpose:** Prove completion and quality

| Shot | What to Include | Why |
|------|-----------------|-----|
| Completed Work | Same angle as "before" shot | Direct comparison |
| Detail | Close-up of finished work | Shows quality and craftsmanship |

**Must Be Visible:**
- Completed repair/installation
- Cleanup done
- Same area as "before" photos
- Any test results (water running, power on, etc.)

## Photo Labeling System

Use descriptive filenames or add notes in the app:

### Naming Convention
```
[STAGE]_[LOCATION]_[DESCRIPTION]
```

### Examples:
- `BEFORE_kitchen_sink_leak`
- `DURING_pipe_replacement`
- `AFTER_sink_repaired_tested`
- `DISCOVERY_rotted_subfloor`

### In-App Notes (Required for):
- Any pre-existing damage
- Discoveries during work
- Deviations from estimate
- Customer-caused issues
- Areas you did NOT work on

## Protective Documentation

### Document These to Protect Yourself:

| Situation | What to Photograph | Notes to Add |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Pre-existing damage | Damage with scale reference | "Pre-existing damage observed on arrival" |
| Customer decisions | Any declined recommendations | "Customer declined [recommendation]" |
| Out of scope areas | Areas near but not part of work | "Adjacent area not part of scope" |
| Difficult access | Obstacles, clutter, conditions | "Access limited by [reason]" |
| Safety concerns | Hazards you're documenting | "Safety concern noted and reported" |

### Verbal Agreements
If customer verbally agrees to anything, document it:
1. Photo of area discussed
2. Note describing agreement
3. Time stamp of conversation
4. Follow up with in-app message confirmation

## Photo Quality Requirements

### Technical Standards

| Requirement | Standard | Why |
|-------------|----------|-----|
| Resolution | Minimum 1080p (1920x1080) | Detail visible when zoomed |
| Focus | Sharp, not blurry | Details must be readable |
| Lighting | Well-lit, details visible | Shadows don't hide problems |
| Framing | Subject fills 60%+ of frame | Clear focus on work area |
| Orientation | Match subject (portrait/landscape) | Professional appearance |

### Common Rejection Reasons

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Blurry/out of focus | Tap to focus, hold steady, retake |
| Too dark | Add light source, use flash carefully |
| Too far away | Get closer, subject should fill frame |
| Obstruction in frame | Move obstacles before shooting |
| Wrong area | Verify you're documenting correct location |
| Missing context | Include wider shot showing location |

## Video Documentation (When Required)

### Required Video For:
- Active water leaks (before stopping)
- HVAC airflow demonstrations
- Drain cleaning (before/after flow)
- Electrical testing (with meter readings)
- Mechanical equipment operation

### Video Standards:
- Duration: 10-30 seconds
- Narrate what you're showing
- Steady camera (no shaking)
- Adequate lighting
- Include test results/readings

# 3. Minimum Required Media by Job Type

## Quick Reference Table

| Job Type | Before (min) | During (min) | After (min) | Special Requirements |
|----------|--------------|--------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Plumbing Leak | 3 | 2 | 3 | Video of leak, water test |
| HVAC | 3 | 2 | 3 | Model/serial, temp readings |
| Electrical | 3 | 2 | 3 | Panel photo, test readings |
| Appliance | 3 | 1 | 2 | Model/serial, error codes |
| Roof/Gutters | 4 | 2 | 4 | Wide + detail, measurements |
| Drywall/Paint | 3 | 2 | 3 | Color match, texture match |
| Flooring | 4 | 2 | 4 | Measurements, pattern match |
| Landscaping | 4 | 2 | 4 | Full area, before/after |

---

## Plumbing Leak

### Before Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Context:** Room/area showing leak location
2. **Problem:** Active leak or damage area
3. **Source:** Where water is coming from (if visible)

### During Photos (2 minimum)
1. **Access:** Opened wall/floor/cabinet showing pipes
2. **Repair:** Replacement parts or repair in progress

### After Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Repair Complete:** Same angle as "before"
2. **Water Test:** Running water, no leaks
3. **Cleanup:** Area restored, dry, clean

### Special Requirements
- **Video (required):** 15-second video of leak BEFORE stopping it
- **Video (required):** Water running test AFTER repair
- **Measurement:** Document extent of water damage
- **Note:** Record water pressure if relevant

### Red Flags to Document
- Mold presence (photograph, do not disturb)
- Rotted subfloor/drywall
- Previous repair attempts
- Corrosion on pipes

---

## HVAC

### Before Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Unit Exterior:** Full view of indoor/outdoor unit
2. **Model/Serial:** Data plate clearly readable
3. **Problem Area:** Specific component if known

### During Photos (2 minimum)
1. **Access Panel Open:** Internal components visible
2. **Work in Progress:** Repair/replacement being performed

### After Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Repair Complete:** Component replaced/repaired
2. **System Running:** Unit operating
3. **Temperature Reading:** Thermostat or temp at vent

### Special Requirements
- **Model/Serial (required):** Must photograph data plate
- **Temperature (required):** Before and after readings
- **Thermostat (required):** Settings and display
- **Filter (required):** Condition and size noted

### Additional for Specific Issues
| Issue | Additional Photos |
|-------|------------------|
| No heat/cool | Thermostat, outdoor unit, error codes |
| Strange noise | Video with audio |
| Leak from unit | Drain pan, condensate line |
| Poor airflow | Vent registers, ductwork access |

---

## Electrical (Outlet/Switch)

### Before Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Context:** Room showing outlet/switch location
2. **Problem:** The specific outlet/switch
3. **Panel:** Main electrical panel (closed, then open)

### During Photos (2 minimum)
1. **Wiring Exposed:** Outlet/switch pulled out showing wiring
2. **New Component:** Replacement part being installed

### After Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Completed:** New outlet/switch installed
2. **Testing:** Voltage tester showing power
3. **Panel:** Panel restored and closed

### Special Requirements
- **Panel Photo (required):** Even for outlet work
- **Voltage Test (required):** Photo of tester showing reading
- **Circuit Identification:** Note which breaker controls it
- **Video (optional):** If demonstrating GFCI test

### Safety Documentation
- Photograph any code violations found
- Document any aluminum wiring discovered
- Note any overloaded circuits
- Photograph any burn marks or damage

---

## Appliance Repair

### Before Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Full Appliance:** Front view showing condition
2. **Model/Serial Tag:** Data plate clearly readable
3. **Problem Indicator:** Error code, damage, or issue area

### During Photos (1 minimum)
1. **Repair Work:** Component being replaced/repaired

### After Photos (2 minimum)
1. **Completed Repair:** Appliance restored
2. **Operational Test:** Appliance running/functioning

### Special Requirements
- **Model/Serial (required):** Must be readable in photo
- **Error Codes:** Photograph display showing any codes
- **Age Verification:** Data plate shows manufacture date

### By Appliance Type
| Appliance | Extra Requirements |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Refrigerator | Temperature reading before/after |
| Washer | Video of test cycle |
| Dryer | Lint trap, vent connection |
| Dishwasher | Video of spray arms running |
| Oven/Range | Temperature test, igniter check |

---

## Roof/Gutters

### Before Photos (4 minimum)
1. **Wide Shot:** Full roof from ground level
2. **Problem Area:** Damaged section (may need zoom)
3. **Gutters:** Full gutter run showing condition
4. **Access Point:** Where you'll access roof

### During Photos (2 minimum)
1. **Repair in Progress:** Work being performed
2. **Materials:** Showing materials being used

### After Photos (4 minimum)
1. **Completed Area:** Same angle as problem before
2. **Detail Shot:** Close-up of repair
3. **Gutters Clean:** If part of scope
4. **Wide Shot:** Full roof after completion

### Special Requirements
- **Measurements (required):** Length/area of damage
- **Shingle Match:** Photo of existing vs replacement
- **Flashing:** Document condition of all flashing
- **Safety:** Document ladder placement and safety measures

### Weather Considerations
- Note weather conditions at time of inspection
- Document any moisture present
- Photograph any areas holding water
- Note if follow-up needed after rain test

---

## Drywall/Paint

### Before Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Room Context:** Full wall showing damage location
2. **Damage Area:** Clear view of hole/crack/damage
3. **Measurement:** Damage with ruler/tape for scale

### During Photos (2 minimum)
1. **Repair Stage:** Patching/mudding in progress
2. **Prep Work:** Surface preparation before paint

### After Photos (3 minimum)
1. **Completed Repair:** Same angle as before
2. **Close-Up:** Texture/finish detail
3. **Color Match:** Shows blend with surrounding area

### Special Requirements
- **Measurements (required):** Size of repair area
- **Texture Match:** Sample of existing texture
- **Paint Code:** Photo of paint can or color match
- **Lighting:** Photos in natural and artificial light

### Quality Indicators to Document
- Smooth finish (no ridges or bumps)
- Texture match (if textured wall)
- Paint blend (no visible edges)
- No flash marks in different lighting

---

## Flooring

### Before Photos (4 minimum)
1. **Room Overview:** Full room showing floor
2. **Damage Area:** Specific area to be repaired
3. **Measurements:** Dimensions of affected area
4. **Existing Material:** Close-up of current flooring

### During Photos (2 minimum)
1. **Removal:** Old flooring removed
2. **Subfloor:** Condition of subfloor (note any issues)

### After Photos (4 minimum)
1. **Room Overview:** Full room with new flooring
2. **Repaired Area:** Same angle as damage before
3. **Transitions:** Where new meets existing
4. **Detail:** Pattern/grain match close-up

### Special Requirements
- **Measurements (required):** Area dimensions
- **Material Match:** Photo of existing and replacement side-by-side
- **Subfloor Condition:** Document before covering
- **Transitions:** All transition points photographed

### By Flooring Type
| Type | Additional Requirements |
|------|------------------------|
| Hardwood | Grain direction, finish type |
| Tile | Grout color, layout pattern |
| Carpet | Pile direction, seam locations |
| Vinyl/LVP | Pattern alignment, expansion gaps |
| Laminate | Expansion gaps, transition strips |

---

## Landscaping

### Before Photos (4 minimum)
1. **Property Overview:** Wide shot of entire area
2. **Work Area:** Specific area to be addressed
3. **Problem Detail:** Overgrowth, damage, or issue
4. **Boundaries:** Property lines, structures, utilities

### During Photos (2 minimum)
1. **Work in Progress:** Activity being performed
2. **Equipment/Materials:** Tools and materials used

### After Photos (4 minimum)
1. **Property Overview:** Same angle as before
2. **Work Area:** Completed area
3. **Detail Shots:** Quality of cuts, edges, installations
4. **Cleanup:** Debris removed, area clean

### Special Requirements
- **Property Boundaries:** Document clearly
- **Utility Markings:** Photo any utility flags
- **Before Height/Size:** Measurements for major trimming
- **Plant Health:** Note any diseased plants found

### Seasonal Considerations
- Note weather conditions
- Document irrigation system status
- Photograph any pest or disease issues
- Note soil conditions if planting
